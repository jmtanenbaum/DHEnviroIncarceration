Featured Stories

Folsom State Prison
Built in 1880, the decrepit Folsom State Prison is infamous for spilling 700,000 gallons of toxic waste into the nearby American River and receiving a $700,000 fine from the EPA.

California Men’s Colony State Prison
Prison administrators dumped 220,00 gallons of raw sewage into Morro Bay in 2004. Even after the prison’s wastewater treatment plant was upgraded, the prison continued to spill sewage into the Bay and was fined by state water officials in 2008 and 2014. Spills continued in 2015 and January of 2016.

Victorville Federal Correctional Complex
This prison complex in San Bernardino County lies on top of a toxic former air force weapon’s facility, which is labeled an ultra hazardous Superfund Site by the EPA.

Kern Valley State Prison
This prison opened in 2005, but soon after state agencies found that drinking water given to incarcerated people contained deadly amounts of known carcinogen arsenic.
